# Athena - Epic Breakdown

## Overview

This document provides the epic and story breakdown for Athena M12, decomposing electrical renderer correctness and professional operator-surface hardening into governed, implementable work above the completed M11 electrical workbench-depth foundation.

## Requirements Inventory

### Functional Requirements

FR-1: Athena can render the first electrical-domain connections in a visually electrical way.
FR-2: Athena can anchor rendered connections to the intended downstream electrical endpoints reliably.
FR-3: Athena can make the first renderer path materially more readable under dense electrical cases.
FR-4: Athena can keep the electrical canvas navigable on denser cases.
FR-5: Athena can preserve a canvas-first electrical operator posture.
FR-6: Athena can present support information in a denser and more professional form.
FR-7: Athena can keep electrical workbench controls compact and legible.
FR-8: Athena can validate M12 through explicit proof repositories or fixtures designed for renderer correctness.
FR-9: Athena can improve renderer correctness without reopening earlier semantic foundations.
FR-10: Athena can navigate from one electrical subject to its related references, destinations, and consequences without losing semantic identity.

### NonFunctional Requirements

NFR-1: Canonical engineering meaning remains upstream of renderer and workbench behavior.
NFR-2: Endpoint and routing behavior must stay explainable against governed projection payloads.
NFR-3: Semantic layer owns endpoint identity, projection owns preferred routing corridor guidance, and renderer owns only the actual visual path.
NFR-4: Denser proof scenes must remain materially more legible than the current generic graph baseline.
NFR-5: Main-canvas focus and panel density must improve operator work rather than add presentation clutter.
NFR-6: M12 must stay focused on renderer correctness and workbench hardening rather than broad new platform scope.
NFR-7: M12 must preserve the existing runtime, LSP, review, and workbench seams instead of creating a second frontend-owned authority.

### Additional Requirements

- M12 builds on completed M11 electrical projection-family, sheet, notation-pack, and repeated-reference contracts rather than reopening them.
- M12 exists to make the first electrical workbench feel like a trusted electrical operator surface rather than a generic node-edge demo.
- Electrical readability must remain downstream of semantic authority.
- Endpoint anchoring must resolve through governed projection anchors or ports rather than renderer-only guesses.
- Routing ownership must stay explicit:
  - semantic layer owns endpoint identity and connection intent
  - projection layer may own preferred routing corridor guidance
  - renderer owns actual visual path only
- Canvas and viewport hardening must remain product-surface work, not a second scene-truth model.
- Cross-reference and related-reveal navigation must resolve through canonical semantic identity first.
- Athena-owned hierarchy, diagnostics, outline, inspector, and graph-side controls should become denser and more IDE-like without stealing focus from the canvas.
- M12 must define at least one larger readability benchmark tier beyond the current M11 dense baseline.
- QElectroTech and EPLAN remain workflow and UX references, not semantic or architectural authorities.
- M12 must preserve M8 mutation authority, M9 knowledge diagnostics and impact, and M10 reasoning surfaces where present.

### UX Design Requirements

None included for M12. Product-surface hardening is in scope, but no separate UX-spec companion is part of this run.

### FR Coverage Map

FR-1: Epic 1 - electrical connection rendering correctness
FR-2: Epic 1 and Epic 5 - endpoint anchoring and identity-safe navigation
FR-3: Epic 1 and Epic 4 - routing readability and benchmark validation
FR-4: Epic 2 - viewport and scene navigation hardening
FR-5: Epic 2 and Epic 3 - canvas-first posture and compact controls
FR-6: Epic 3 - professional Athena panel density
FR-7: Epic 2 and Epic 3 - compact graph-side controls and additive workbench polish
FR-8: Epic 4 - renderer-correct proof fixtures and explicit limits
FR-9: Epic 1, Epic 4, and Epic 5 - preserve semantic, review, knowledge, and reasoning coherence
FR-10: Epic 5 - electrical cross-reference navigation and related reveal

## Epic 1: Electrical Connection Rendering Correctness

Engineers can read electrical connections as conductor intent with stable endpoint anchoring and governed routing ownership, without moving connection truth into renderer state.

### Story 1.1: Publish Governed Electrical Anchor And Routing-Corridor Contracts

As a platform engineer,
I want Athena to publish explicit downstream anchor and routing-corridor contracts,
So that electrical connection rendering can improve without making route geometry or renderer guesses the source of truth.

**Implements:** FR-1, FR-2, FR-3, FR-9

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** the completed M11 electrical projection-family foundation
**When** M12 introduces connection-rendering hardening
**Then** Athena publishes governed electrical anchor or port contracts and optional preferred routing-corridor guidance
**And** those contracts remain downstream of canonical engineering identity

**Given** routing ownership is reviewed for architecture drift
**When** the boundary is inspected
**Then** semantic layer owns endpoint identity, projection owns corridor guidance, and renderer owns visual path only
**And** rendered route geometry does not become engineering truth

### Story 1.2: Deliver Conductor-First Electrical Connection Rendering

As an engineer,
I want electrical connections to read like wiring or cabinet linkage rather than generic graph edges,
So that the main canvas looks and feels like electrical work instead of a node-edge demo.

**Implements:** FR-1, FR-3

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** one dense electrical view is rendered
**When** Athena draws connection relationships
**Then** the connection output is visually distinguishable as electrical conductor intent rather than a generic graph line
**And** labels, markers, or similar connection cues remain visible enough to support inspection

**Given** routing or overlap quality is inspected on a denser scene
**When** the rendered result is compared with the current generic baseline
**Then** the scene is materially more readable
**And** readability improvements do not require renderer-local semantic inference

### Story 1.3: Preserve Stable Endpoint Anchoring And Selection Coherence

As an engineer,
I want rendered electrical connections to terminate at the intended endpoints,
So that selection, reveal, and inspection remain trustworthy under denser views.

**Implements:** FR-2, FR-9

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** a rendered connection originates from governed projection anchors
**When** Athena draws and later selects that connection
**Then** the rendered endpoints remain traceable to the intended subjects, ports, or terminals
**And** endpoint selection does not degrade into approximate node-center behavior only

**Given** a rendered endpoint is inspected through selection or reveal
**When** Athena resolves the subject
**Then** the resolved identity remains canonical and coherent with runtime, review, and knowledge paths
**And** the renderer does not introduce a parallel endpoint-identity model

## Epic 2: Canvas And Viewport Hardening

Engineers can fit, pan, zoom, and focus dense electrical scenes reliably while the graph surface remains the dominant work area.

### Story 2.1: Derive Reliable Scene Bounds And Fit-To-Viewport Behavior

As an engineer,
I want fit-to-viewport to land on the actual electrical scene reliably,
So that I do not have to hunt the graph manually after basic viewport actions.

**Implements:** FR-4, FR-5

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** a dense electrical scene is loaded
**When** the operator invokes fit-to-viewport
**Then** Athena centers and scales against the real rendered scene bounds
**And** the operator can immediately see the main electrical content

**Given** viewport behavior is reviewed for architecture drift
**When** scene-bound and focus logic are inspected
**Then** those calculations remain downstream product-surface behavior
**And** no second frontend-owned scene-truth model is introduced

### Story 2.2: Harden Pan, Zoom, Focus, And Canvas Expansion Behavior

As an engineer,
I want panning, zooming, focus, and panel-driven canvas expansion to remain usable on larger scenes,
So that the graph stays the main work surface during dense electrical work.

**Implements:** FR-4, FR-5, FR-7

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** the operator pans or zooms a larger electrical scene
**When** Athena updates the viewport
**Then** orientation remains usable without immediately losing the active area
**And** focus behavior does not strand the operator away from the meaningful scene

**Given** support panels are opened, collapsed, or closed
**When** the workbench recomposes the main layout
**Then** the graph surface expands or contracts predictably
**And** the graph remains the dominant main-panel surface

### Story 2.3: Keep Floating Graph Controls Compact And Non-Intrusive

As an engineer,
I want graph-side tools to stay compact and legible,
So that graph controls help navigation without visually fighting the electrical scene.

**Implements:** FR-5, FR-7

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** graph-side controls are visible around the canvas
**When** the operator uses or hovers them
**Then** controls expose clear meaning with compact icon-led presentation where appropriate
**And** they do not consume disproportionate visual attention

**Given** floating controls or overlays are reviewed
**When** the product surface is inspected
**Then** the controls remain subordinate to the main canvas
**And** they do not create a second mode or state authority outside the existing workbench seams

## Epic 3: Professional Electrical Workbench Density

Engineers can use denser Athena-owned hierarchy, diagnostics, outline, inspector, and graph-side control surfaces that feel like a serious IDE and ECAD workbench rather than a demo shell.

### Story 3.1: Harden Athena-Owned Panels To Compact IDE-Style Density

As an engineer,
I want Athena-owned support panels to be denser and easier to scan,
So that hierarchy, diagnostics, outline, and inspection surfaces feel professional under electrical project load.

**Implements:** FR-6

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** Athena-owned hierarchy, diagnostics, outline, or inspection views are rendered
**When** the operator scans those surfaces
**Then** compact IDE-style lists, trees, or tables are preferred over noisy demo-card layouts
**And** information density improves without weakening active-context readability

**Given** the panel surfaces are reviewed for product quality
**When** framing and spacing are inspected
**Then** repeated borders, oversized empty states, and decorative panel chrome are reduced
**And** the change remains additive inside the current workbench shell

### Story 3.2: Make Electrical Context Panels Coherent With Canvas-First Operation

As an engineer,
I want support panels and electrical context surfaces to stay coherent with the active canvas,
So that the workbench feels like one operator surface instead of a set of unrelated demo panes.

**Implements:** FR-6, FR-7

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** the operator changes selection or active view on the canvas
**When** related support panels refresh
**Then** hierarchy, diagnostics, outline, and inspector surfaces stay aligned to the same canonical context
**And** the panel refresh path remains additive through existing runtime and LSP seams

**Given** graph-side status or mode surfaces are shown
**When** the operator inspects their design
**Then** those controls remain compact and coherent with the denser IDE-like panel language
**And** they do not pull the visual center away from the canvas

## Epic 4: Renderer Benchmark Proof And Coherence

Architecture owners can validate M12 on larger readability benchmark tiers while preserving mutation, review, knowledge, and reasoning coherence.

### Story 4.1: Publish Larger Readability Benchmark Tiers And Renderer-Correct Proof Fixtures

As an architecture owner,
I want Athena to ship explicit renderer-readability benchmark tiers and proof fixtures,
So that M12 is validated on more than one dense pretty demo.

**Implements:** FR-3, FR-8

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** M12 renderer-hardening work exists
**When** Athena publishes the milestone proof corpus
**Then** it includes at least one readability benchmark tier materially larger than the M11 dense baseline
**And** the benchmark records device or wire count plus explicit readability and interaction checks

**Given** product or architecture review is performed
**When** the proof fixtures are exercised
**Then** known visual and interaction limits are documented explicitly
**And** M12 does not claim renderer trust from a single toy or beauty-shot case only

### Story 4.2: Preserve Mutation, Review, Knowledge, And Reasoning Coherence Under Renderer Hardening

As a reviewer,
I want renderer hardening to stay coherent with Athena's existing mutation, review, knowledge, and reasoning paths,
So that better visuals do not fracture the platform authority model.

**Implements:** FR-9

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** a denser renderer-hardened electrical view is active
**When** selection, reveal, mutation review, knowledge diagnostics, or reasoning surfaces are used
**Then** they remain anchored to canonical semantic identity
**And** no renderer-local semantic reconstruction layer is introduced

**Given** a change originates from source or graph under the M12 fixture
**When** Athena processes the resulting update
**Then** the existing M8 mutation authority and M6 or M9 or M10 downstream consequences remain intact
**And** the renderer hardening path does not require a separate review or reasoning vocabulary

### Story 4.3: Publish Explicit Visual Validation Path And Limit Recording

As an architecture owner,
I want M12 to record what visual trust has and has not been proven,
So that later milestones inherit clear renderer boundaries instead of marketing claims.

**Implements:** FR-8, FR-9

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** M12 closeout materials are reviewed
**When** the milestone documents are read
**Then** they explicitly state what connection readability, viewport behavior, navigation trust, and benchmark scale were proven
**And** they explicitly record what remains later than M12

**Given** future planning uses M12 as a base
**When** later ECAD milestones are scoped
**Then** they can distinguish renderer-trust work already proven from deeper parity or workflow work still deferred
**And** M12 does not need to be reinterpreted from implementation folklore

## Epic 5: Electrical Navigation And Reference Surface

Engineers can navigate from one electrical subject to related references, destinations, diagnostics, and semantic subjects without losing canonical identity.

### Story 5.1: Publish Governed Electrical Navigation Contracts

As a platform engineer,
I want Athena to define explicit navigation and related-reveal contracts for electrical subjects,
So that cross-reference movement is identity-safe and not built on renderer-local object ids.

**Implements:** FR-2, FR-10

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** repeated references, sheets, and canonical subject identities already exist from M11
**When** M12 introduces richer electrical navigation
**Then** Athena publishes governed navigation or reveal contracts for at least one first flow such as coil-to-contact, source-to-destination, or subject-to-repeated-reference
**And** those contracts resolve through canonical identity first

**Given** navigation contracts are inspected for architecture drift
**When** the boundary is reviewed
**Then** renderer-local object ids and widget ids remain aliases only
**And** navigation truth remains in existing runtime, projection, and reference contracts

### Story 5.2: Deliver Cross-Reference Navigation And Related Reveal In The Workbench

As an engineer,
I want to jump from one electrical subject to related references, diagnostics, or semantic subjects,
So that the workbench behaves more like a professional electrical tool than a static graph viewer.

**Implements:** FR-10, FR-9

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** one electrical subject has related references or consequences
**When** the operator requests related navigation from the workbench
**Then** Athena can reveal at least one governed cross-reference path such as coil-to-contact, source-to-destination, or repeated-reference jump
**And** the resolved destination remains semantically coherent with the originating subject

**Given** a subject also has diagnostics, impact, or related engineering context
**When** the operator requests related reveal
**Then** Athena can surface those related items through the same canonical subject path
**And** navigation does not create a separate electrical-navigation authority outside current seams
