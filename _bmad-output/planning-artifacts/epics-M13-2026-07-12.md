# Athena - Epic Breakdown

## Overview

This document provides the epic and story breakdown for Athena M13, decomposing the new presentation-language foundation into governed, implementable work above the completed M12 renderer-hardening milestone.

M13 is a foundation milestone, not a surface-polish continuation. Its job is to establish a real downstream presentation language through `Presentation IR`, primitive presentation packs, composite presentation packs, and backend abstraction, while keeping semantic macro or engineering assembly explicitly outside presentation ownership.

## Requirements Inventory

### Functional Requirements

FR-1: Athena can derive a dedicated `Presentation IR` from projection contracts.
FR-2: Athena can keep `Presentation IR` downstream of semantic and projection authority.
FR-3: Athena can publish a minimal primitive presentation library.
FR-4: Athena can keep primitive presentation style-driven and pack-driven.
FR-5: Athena can support composite presentation definitions above primitive atoms.
FR-6: Athena can allow one canonical subject to project into multiple composite variants.
FR-7: Athena can keep presentation composition separate from semantic macro composition.
FR-8: Athena can preserve semantic traceability across composite occurrences.
FR-9: Athena can support multiple renderer backends over one `Presentation IR`.
FR-10: Athena can host presentation packs through extension contracts.

### NonFunctional Requirements

NFR-1: Canonical engineering meaning remains in `Engineering IR`.
NFR-2: `Projection Model` remains the renderer-neutral view contract.
NFR-3: `Presentation IR` remains rebuildable from upstream artifacts and may not become a second semantic core.
NFR-4: Electrical is the first serious presentation pack, but the top-level architecture name remains domain-neutral.
NFR-5: Renderer backends remain replaceable and may not redefine primitives, composites, or semantic identity.
NFR-6: M13 must not smuggle semantic macro or engineering assembly into presentation ownership.
NFR-7: Mutation, review, knowledge, reveal, and AI-context flows must stay coherent across presentation occurrences.

### Additional Requirements

- M13 builds on completed M11 electrical projection-family, sheet, notation-pack, and repeated-reference contracts.
- M13 builds on completed M12 renderer-hardening and renderer-foundation work instead of reopening it.
- `Presentation IR` exists to prevent every client from reconstructing presentation ad hoc from generic node-edge payloads.
- Primitive definitions remain downstream assets, not semantic truth.
- Composite presentation remains downstream assembly, not engineering composition.
- If Athena later introduces semantic macro or engineering assembly, that layer must live above projection and then project down into `Presentation IR`.
- The first proof should stay narrow: one `Presentation IR` contract, one primitive electrical pack, one composite electrical pack, one end-to-end proof repository, and one proof backend.
- SVG proof rendering remains valid for M13 as long as the backend stays replaceable.
- Theia remains the workbench shell, and GLSP remains the preferred diagram-architecture pattern; neither becomes the presentation-language authority.

### UX Design Requirements

None included for M13. M13 is primarily a downstream presentation-language and backend-boundary milestone, not a standalone UX-spec milestone.

### FR Coverage Map

FR-1: Epic 1 - Presentation IR foundation
FR-2: Epic 1 and Epic 5 - downstream authority and semantic traceability
FR-3: Epic 2 - primitive presentation packs
FR-4: Epic 2 and Epic 4 - style-driven packs and backend independence
FR-5: Epic 3 - composite presentation system
FR-6: Epic 3 and Epic 5 - multi-variant presentation and identity-safe occurrences
FR-7: Epic 5 - semantic macro boundary
FR-8: Epic 5 - reveal/review/diagnostic coherence across occurrences
FR-9: Epic 4 - backend abstraction and proof backend path
FR-10: Epic 2 and Epic 4 - extension-compatible presentation packs

## Epic 1: Presentation IR Foundation

Platform engineers can derive and publish a neutral `Presentation IR` from projection contracts so presentation richness stops being reconstructed ad hoc inside clients.

### Story 1.1: Define The Neutral Presentation IR Contract

As a platform engineer,  
I want Athena to define a domain-neutral `Presentation IR`,  
So that electrical can be the first serious pack without freezing the architecture around one domain name.

**Implements:** FR-1, FR-2

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** M13 introduces the new downstream layer  
**When** the contract is reviewed  
**Then** the top-level model is named `Presentation IR` rather than an electrical-only name  
**And** the contract remains suitable for future domain packs such as electrical, SCADA, or documentation

**Given** the layer boundary is inspected  
**When** architecture owners trace ownership  
**Then** `Engineering IR` remains semantic truth, `Projection Model` remains view contract, and `Presentation IR` remains downstream language only

### Story 1.2: Derive Presentation IR From Existing Projection Contracts

As a platform engineer,  
I want Athena to derive `Presentation IR` from existing projection-family, sheet, notation, anchor, and routing contracts,  
So that presentation richness is rebuildable and inspectable.

**Implements:** FR-1, FR-2

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** one supported electrical projection view  
**When** Athena derives the new presentation layer  
**Then** the result reuses upstream sheet, notation, anchor, endpoint, and routing guidance instead of re-inventing them in the frontend

**Given** the derivation is inspected for drift  
**When** reviewers compare upstream and downstream contracts  
**Then** `Presentation IR` can be rebuilt from upstream artifacts  
**And** no second semantic authority is introduced

### Story 1.3: Publish Presentation IR Through Existing Runtime And Transport Seams

As a platform engineer,  
I want the new layer to flow through runtime and IDE transport using existing seams,  
So that M13 strengthens the architecture without bypassing current product boundaries.

**Implements:** FR-1, FR-2

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** M13 presentation output is exposed to clients  
**When** the transport path is reviewed  
**Then** the new layer is delivered through existing runtime, LSP, and workbench boundaries

**Given** the product surface requests presentation state  
**When** the request is served  
**Then** frontend code consumes Athena-owned presentation payloads rather than inferring them from graph widgets alone

## Epic 2: Primitive Presentation Pack Foundation

Engineers can express first serious electrical presentation through governed primitive packs instead of generic graph fallback.

### Story 2.1: Define The Primitive Presentation Atom Vocabulary

As a platform engineer,  
I want Athena to define primitive presentation atoms with stable ids and slots,  
So that downstream presentation starts from reusable language instead of hardcoded widgets.

**Implements:** FR-3, FR-4

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** M13 introduces primitive presentation  
**When** the primitive contract is reviewed  
**Then** primitives expose stable ids, anchors, slots, token references, and optional orientation rules

**Given** primitives are checked for semantic drift  
**When** reviewers inspect their responsibilities  
**Then** primitives remain presentation assets only  
**And** they do not define engineering truth

### Story 2.2: Publish The First Electrical Primitive Presentation Pack

As an electrical engineer,  
I want Athena to ship a first serious electrical primitive pack,  
So that core electrical subjects stop depending on generic card and line fallback as the primary posture.

**Implements:** FR-3, FR-4

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** the first M13 proof pack is implemented  
**When** electrical core subjects are rendered through it  
**Then** the pack can express at least contact marks, coils, terminals, breaker or motor marks, conductor furniture, and related reference marks

**Given** the pack is reviewed for product evolution  
**When** future style variation is considered  
**Then** primitive identity stays separate from final visual skin and notation style

### Story 2.3: Make Primitive Packs Extension-Compatible

As a platform engineer,  
I want primitive packs to be modeled as extension-compatible assets,  
So that future vendors, industries, or house styles can add new packs without rewriting the core layer.

**Implements:** FR-4, FR-10

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** a primitive pack is published  
**When** the pack contract is reviewed  
**Then** it is hosted through Athena-owned extension-friendly boundaries rather than one frontend-only hardcode path

**Given** future alternate packs are considered  
**When** the architecture is inspected  
**Then** pack registration, versioning, and replacement remain feasible without changing semantic core contracts

## Epic 3: Composite Presentation System

Engineers can assemble richer downstream subject presentation from primitive atoms while preserving one canonical semantic subject.

### Story 3.1: Define Composite Presentation Contracts

As a platform engineer,  
I want Athena to define composite presentation contracts above primitive atoms,  
So that richer subject presentation becomes governed and reusable.

**Implements:** FR-5, FR-6

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** M13 introduces composite presentation  
**When** the composite contract is reviewed  
**Then** composites can define parts, text slots, terminal slots, anchor mappings, and local layout rules

**Given** composite ownership is inspected  
**When** architecture owners compare it with semantic layers  
**Then** composites remain downstream presentation assemblies  
**And** do not become canonical engineering models

### Story 3.2: Support Multiple Composite Variants For One Canonical Subject

As an engineer,  
I want one canonical subject to present through different composite variants by family or pack,  
So that Athena can support multiple operator views without identity drift.

**Implements:** FR-6

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** one canonical subject appears across more than one supported view family  
**When** presentation variants differ  
**Then** the subject remains one canonical semantic identity  
**And** the selected composite variant remains governed by downstream contracts

**Given** reveal or selection is triggered from any variant  
**When** Athena resolves the subject  
**Then** the result remains canonical-identity-first

### Story 3.3: Publish The First Composite Electrical Presentation Pack

As an electrical engineer,  
I want Athena to ship a first composite electrical presentation pack,  
So that richer device presentation can be proven above the primitive vocabulary.

**Implements:** FR-5, FR-6

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** the first M13 proof pack is built  
**When** at least one richer electrical subject is rendered  
**Then** its presentation is assembled from governed primitive atoms through a published composite contract

**Given** the composite pack is inspected  
**When** reviewers trace its mappings  
**Then** canonical ports or terminals remain traceable to explicit presentation anchors

## Epic 4: Renderer Backend Abstraction And Proof Path

Renderer engineers can feed one `Presentation IR` into at least one proof backend while preserving a future-main-backend path.

### Story 4.1: Define The Renderer Backend Interface Above Presentation IR

As a renderer engineer,  
I want Athena to define a backend seam above `Presentation IR`,  
So that backend choice stays replaceable.

**Implements:** FR-9

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** M13 introduces the backend seam  
**When** the interface is reviewed  
**Then** backend-specific scene trees, draw commands, and batching concerns remain backend-owned

**Given** backend replacement is considered  
**When** the architecture is inspected  
**Then** changing backend does not redefine primitives, composites, or semantic identity

### Story 4.2: Deliver One Proof Backend Over Presentation IR

As a renderer engineer,  
I want Athena to prove the new layer through one real backend,  
So that M13 is validated end to end instead of staying conceptual.

**Implements:** FR-9

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** the first M13 proof repository  
**When** it is rendered through the selected proof backend  
**Then** the backend consumes `Presentation IR` rather than generic graph fallback as the primary presentation path

**Given** the proof backend is inspected  
**When** product owners review the stack  
**Then** SVG remains acceptable as a proof and debug backend  
**And** the architecture still preserves a future-main-backend path

### Story 4.3: Keep Pack And Backend Contracts Independent

As a platform engineer,  
I want pack contracts and backend contracts to stay independent,  
So that presentation-language growth does not lock Athena to one temporary renderer.

**Implements:** FR-4, FR-9, FR-10

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** a primitive or composite pack is reviewed  
**When** backend-specific assumptions are checked  
**Then** pack structure does not encode one backend's internal draw tree

**Given** a backend consumes a pack  
**When** alternate backends are considered  
**Then** the same pack remains consumable through Athena-owned contracts

## Epic 5: Semantic Traceability And Composition Boundary

Architecture owners can preserve mutation, review, diagnostics, reveal, and AI-context coherence across presentation occurrences while keeping semantic macro out of presentation ownership.

### Story 5.1: Preserve Canonical Traceability Across Primitive And Composite Occurrences

As an architecture owner,  
I want primitive and composite occurrences to remain traceable to canonical subjects,  
So that presentation richness does not fork review, diagnostics, or mutation meaning.

**Implements:** FR-2, FR-8

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** a canonical subject appears through multiple presentation occurrences  
**When** selection, reveal, or diagnostics are triggered  
**Then** Athena resolves canonical semantic identity first and presentation occurrence identity second

**Given** mutation or review is inspected  
**When** presentation occurrences are involved  
**Then** they do not create a second subject-identity model

### Story 5.2: Publish The Composition Boundary Between Presentation And Semantic Macro

As an architecture owner,  
I want Athena to explicitly separate presentation composition from future semantic macro or engineering assembly,  
So that M13 does not absorb the wrong responsibility.

**Implements:** FR-7

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** M13 planning and architecture are reviewed  
**When** the composition boundary is inspected  
**Then** M13 clearly excludes semantic macro or engineering assembly from `Presentation IR`

**Given** future reusable engineering composition is discussed  
**When** the milestone boundary is checked  
**Then** the future layer is placed above projection and projected down into `Presentation IR`

### Story 5.3: Prove Coherence Across Review, Knowledge, And AI Context Surfaces

As a product owner,  
I want richer presentation occurrences to remain coherent with existing review, knowledge, and AI surfaces,  
So that M13 strengthens the product foundation rather than fragmenting it.

**Implements:** FR-2, FR-8

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** one proof repository uses primitive and composite presentation  
**When** review, diagnostics, reveal, or AI-context assembly are exercised  
**Then** the outputs remain canonical-subject coherent

**Given** the new presentation layer is inspected  
**When** the existing M8 through M10 product paths are reviewed  
**Then** `Presentation IR` remains downstream and additive rather than introducing a parallel product authority
