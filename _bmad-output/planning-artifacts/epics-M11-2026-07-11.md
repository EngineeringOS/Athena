# Athena - Epic Breakdown

## Overview

This document provides the epic and story breakdown for Athena M11, decomposing the first serious electrical ECAD workbench-depth milestone into implementable user-value-centered epics.

## Requirements Inventory

### Functional Requirements

FR-1: Athena can represent the first ECAD domain through richer electrical projection families.
FR-2: Athena can preserve one semantic identity across richer electrical workbench views.
FR-3: Athena can support the first governed sheet or page model for electrical workbench operation.
FR-4: Athena can support the first governed electrical symbol and notation pack boundary.
FR-5: Athena can display repeated references and cross-reference relationships for the first ECAD domain.
FR-6: Athena can support a denser electrical workbench under realistic information load.
FR-7: Athena can validate the first serious electrical workbench through published larger proof fixtures.
FR-8: Athena can keep earlier milestone guarantees intact while deepening the electrical workbench.
FR-9: Athena can deepen electrical product behavior without shifting the architecture center into the renderer.

### NonFunctional Requirements

NFR-1: Canonical engineering meaning remains upstream of sheet, symbol, notation, and renderer behavior.
NFR-2: The same engineering subject must remain stable across repeated references, sheets, views, diagnostics, and review contexts.
NFR-3: The first serious electrical proof must remain usable at denser graph and panel loads than earlier proof cases.
NFR-4: The boundary between engineering entity, projection rule, sheet structure, and notation pack remains inspectable for architecture and debugging.
NFR-5: The first ECAD-depth milestone must stay focused on serious electrical workbench depth rather than turning into a full downstream product clone.
NFR-6: M11 must preserve the existing runtime, LSP, review, and workbench seams instead of creating a second frontend-owned authority.

### Additional Requirements

- M11 builds on the completed M0 through M9 foundations and the planned M10 AI-assisted reasoning boundary rather than reopening kernel, mutation, or knowledge ownership.
- M11 must deepen the first electrical ECAD workbench without turning Athena into a full EPLAN clone, procurement suite, standards platform, or unrestricted CAD tool.
- Canonical `Engineering IR` remains the source of engineering truth; richer electrical views stay downstream of canonical engineering entities.
- M11 must define explicit electrical projection families for at least schematic, cabinet, wiring, and documentation-oriented views.
- Sheet identity must remain projection-owned and separate from engineering identity.
- Symbol and notation packs must remain governed, inspectable, extension-compatible downstream contracts.
- Repeated references and cross references must always resolve through canonical semantic identity first.
- Dense electrical proof cases are a mandatory architecture contract, not optional milestone polish.
- Denser workbench behavior must stay additive through existing runtime-owned projection sessions, `ide/lsp`, and current workbench surfaces.
- New electrical workbench behavior must remain coherent with the M8 mutation path, the M6 semantic review path, the M9 knowledge-runtime path, and the planned M10 AI path.
- QElectroTech and EPLAN are workflow and UX references, not semantic or architectural authorities.
- The first realistic proof should include a larger electrical repository with repeated patterns, more than 10 components, more than 20 connections, at least one explicit sheet structure, at least one cross-reference pattern, and denser label and inspection load.
- M11 should explicitly improve fit-to-view reliability, reveal and selection coherence, outline and hierarchy usefulness, and property and inspection readability on denser cases.
- M11 must keep known scale, density, and notation limits explicit in the proof corpus and documentation.

### UX Design Requirements

None included for M11. The older UX pair was intentionally excluded from this run.

### FR Coverage Map

FR-1: Epic 1 - richer electrical projection families
FR-2: Epic 1 - canonical identity across electrical views
FR-3: Epic 1 - explicit sheet model
FR-4: Epic 1 - governed symbol and notation boundary
FR-5: Epic 2 - repeated references and cross-reference display
FR-6: Epic 2 - denser electrical workbench interaction
FR-7: Epic 2 - large-case electrical proof fixtures
FR-8: Epic 2 - mutation, review, knowledge, and AI-path coherence under ECAD depth
FR-9: Epic 2 - renderer remains downstream of canonical semantic authority

## Epic List

Engineers can work with one canonical electrical design through richer schematic, cabinet, wiring, and documentation-oriented views with explicit sheets and governed notation, without creating parallel truths.
**FRs covered:** FR-1, FR-2, FR-3, FR-4

### Epic 2: Dense Electrical Workbench And Coherent Review
Engineers and reviewers can operate on larger electrical repositories with repeated references, denser interaction, published proof fixtures, and preserved mutation, knowledge, and review coherence while keeping the renderer downstream of semantics.

## Epic 1: Serious Electrical View Foundations

Engineers can work with one canonical electrical design through richer schematic, cabinet, wiring, and documentation-oriented views with explicit sheets and governed notation, without creating parallel truths.

### Story 1.1: Publish Electrical Projection Family Contracts

As a platform engineer,
I want Athena to define explicit electrical projection-family contracts above canonical engineering entities,
So that schematic, cabinet, wiring, and documentation views can exist without creating parallel semantic models.

**Implements:** FR-1, FR-2

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** the completed M7 to M9 projection and runtime spine
**When** M11 introduces richer electrical view families
**Then** Athena publishes explicit downstream contracts for at least schematic, cabinet, wiring, and documentation-oriented projection families
**And** those contracts resolve back to canonical engineering identities rather than view-local entities

**Given** one engineering subject appears in more than one projection family
**When** reveal or inspection is requested
**Then** Athena can identify the same canonical subject across those families
**And** no projection family becomes a second semantic authority

### Story 1.2: Introduce The First Governed Electrical Sheet Model

As an engineer,
I want Athena to support stable electrical sheets or pages,
So that I can navigate denser electrical documentation without turning page structure into engineering truth.

**Implements:** FR-3, FR-2

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** an electrical project with governed downstream views
**When** Athena constructs sheet-aware representations
**Then** each sheet has stable identity, ordering, and navigation semantics
**And** sheet identity remains separate from engineering identity

**Given** the same engineering subject appears on one or more sheets
**When** the workbench resolves selection or navigation
**Then** Athena preserves the canonical subject anchor across those sheets
**And** sheet membership does not redefine the meaning of the subject

### Story 1.3: Introduce Governed Electrical Symbol And Notation Packs

As a platform engineer,
I want Athena to define governed symbol and notation-pack contracts,
So that electrical symbol choice, labels, and markers stay inspectable, reusable, and downstream of semantics.

**Implements:** FR-4, FR-2

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** canonical electrical entities and projection-family outputs
**When** Athena selects symbols, labels, or notation behavior
**Then** those choices come from governed notation-pack contracts rather than renderer-local hardcoding
**And** the resulting mappings remain inspectable and extension-compatible

**Given** notation behavior is reviewed for architectural drift
**When** the boundary is inspected
**Then** symbol choice and labels remain downstream presentation rules
**And** engineering truth continues to live in canonical semantic state

### Story 1.4: Deliver Multi-View Electrical Outputs Through Runtime And LSP

As an engineer,
I want Athena to publish richer electrical views, sheets, and notation through the existing runtime and LSP path,
So that the first serious ECAD foundation reaches the current product shell without introducing a second frontend-owned authority.

**Implements:** FR-1, FR-3, FR-4

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** projection-family, sheet, and notation contracts are available
**When** Athena publishes them to the workbench
**Then** delivery flows through runtime-owned projection sessions and `ide/lsp`
**And** the workbench consumes those governed outputs without inventing separate semantic state

**Given** an electrical projection family is delivered to the workbench
**When** the transport contract is inspected
**Then** each delivered payload identifies the canonical subject and downstream electrical view family it represents
**And** downstream consumers can render the view without reconstructing engineering meaning from renderer-local data

### Story 1.5: Preserve Reveal And Inspection Coherence Across Delivered Electrical Views

As an engineer,
I want canonical subjects to stay revealable and inspectable across source, graph, and sheet contexts,
So that richer electrical views do not create parallel navigation or identity paths.

**Implements:** FR-2

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** the same canonical subject appears in source, graph, and one or more sheet-aware electrical views
**When** Athena resolves reveal or inspection for that subject
**Then** the same canonical identity can be located across those contexts
**And** no frontend-local alias map becomes the source of truth for subject identity

**Given** a user selects the subject from any one supported context
**When** Athena updates inspector and navigation state
**Then** the resolved selection remains consistent across the other supported contexts
**And** repeated or alternate view entries do not create duplicate semantic subjects

## Epic 2: Dense Electrical Workbench And Coherent Review

Engineers and reviewers can operate on larger electrical repositories with repeated references, denser interaction, published proof fixtures, and preserved mutation, knowledge, and review coherence while keeping the renderer downstream of semantics.

### Story 2.1: Support Repeated References And Cross-Reference Resolution

As an engineer,
I want Athena to show repeated references and cross-reference relationships for electrical subjects,
So that I can understand the same engineering entity across multiple sheets and representations without semantic drift.

**Implements:** FR-5, FR-8

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** a canonical electrical subject appears in more than one downstream representation
**When** Athena publishes repeated-reference or cross-reference data
**Then** each displayed reference resolves back to the same canonical semantic identity
**And** repeated symbol instances do not become independent semantic entities

**Given** diagnostics, inspection, or review is requested for a repeated subject
**When** Athena resolves the context
**Then** the same subject remains coherent across repeated references, sheets, and views
**And** cross-reference display remains compatible with existing review and inspection paths

**Given** a repeated reference cannot be resolved unambiguously
**When** Athena evaluates the cross-reference state
**Then** it surfaces an explicit unresolved or ambiguous-reference outcome
**And** Athena does not invent a fallback alias identity to hide the failure

### Story 2.2: Publish A Larger Electrical Proof Repository And Verification Path

As an architecture owner,
I want Athena to ship a larger electrical proof corpus with explicit verification steps,
So that M11 proves serious ECAD workbench depth on realistic fixtures rather than toy graphs.

**Implements:** FR-7, FR-6

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** the first M11 electrical workbench features are implemented
**When** Athena publishes the milestone proof corpus
**Then** it includes at least one larger electrical repository or equivalent dense fixture with repeated patterns, explicit sheet structure, and cross-reference behavior
**And** the corpus materially exceeds earlier proof density

**Given** product or architecture review is performed
**When** the proof corpus is executed
**Then** the milestone provides repeatable verification steps for rendering, navigation, selection, reveal, and coherence
**And** known limits are documented explicitly rather than hidden

### Story 2.3: Improve Dense Electrical Workbench Navigation And Reveal

As an engineer,
I want Athena to stay navigable on the published M11 dense proof fixture,
So that fit-to-view, selection, reveal, and inspection still work under realistic information density.

**Implements:** FR-6, FR-8

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** the published M11 dense proof fixture with more than 10 components and more than 20 connections
**When** the workbench renders and navigates the project
**Then** core operations such as fit-to-view, navigation, selection, reveal, and inspection remain usable
**And** the workbench does not collapse into narrow demo-only behavior on that fixture

**Given** the same fixture contains heavier label, property, and reference load
**When** the operator switches views or reveals a subject
**Then** Athena preserves usable focus, readable context, and stable reveal behavior
**And** the denser interaction remains additive through existing workbench surfaces rather than a new workbench model

### Story 2.4: Preserve Mutation, Review, And Knowledge Coherence Under ECAD Depth

As a reviewer,
I want richer electrical workbench behavior to stay coherent with Athena's existing mutation, review, and knowledge paths,
So that deeper downstream workbench features do not fracture the platform authority model.

**Implements:** FR-8, FR-9

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** a change originates from source, graph, sheet, or repeated-reference context
**When** Athena processes the resulting update
**Then** the change continues to flow through the M8 mutation authority and the existing semantic review path
**And** no separate workbench-local mutation model is introduced

**Given** M9 knowledge diagnostics or later semantic consumers inspect richer electrical views
**When** those outputs are surfaced against richer electrical views
**Then** they remain anchored to canonical semantic identities across sheets and repeated references
**And** M11 does not introduce a separate compatibility layer beyond the existing canonical semantic contracts

### Story 2.5: Keep The Renderer Downstream Under Serious Electrical Depth

As a platform engineer,
I want Athena to deliver serious electrical workbench depth without moving semantic ownership into frontend or renderer state,
So that M11 strengthens the product while preserving the semantic-layer strategy.

**Implements:** FR-9, FR-8

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**Given** M11 adds richer sheets, notation, repeated references, and denser workbench interaction
**When** the architecture boundary is inspected
**Then** renderer and workbench layers remain consumers of governed projection and runtime outputs
**And** no renderer-local semantics model is required to operate the first serious ECAD proof

**Given** QElectroTech or EPLAN-inspired workflows influence the product shape
**When** those behaviors are implemented in Athena
**Then** they remain downstream UX and workflow references only
**And** they do not redefine Athena's canonical semantic authority
