# Epic 6 Retrospective: Demo Sample And Product Proof

## Epic Summary

Epic 6 turned M30 into a reviewable demo path:

- Story 6.1 created the semantic M30 rolling-shutter sample project.
- Story 6.2 wired structured product smoke around the sample and its representation proof.
- Story 6.3 added screenshot evidence and reviewer usage documentation.

## What Worked

- The sample stayed semantic-source only: no visual geometry, no QET runtime dependency, no sheet file confusion.
- Structured proof covered the actual product concerns: representation library ids, binding counts, anchor usage, route anchors, composition bounds, and transparent chrome.
- Screenshot evidence remained secondary and human-inspectable instead of becoming the only acceptance authority.

## What Needed Discipline

- Product smoke was vulnerable to async UI model timing until the Outline proof explicitly waited for the tree to populate.
- Demo documentation needed to stay coupled to the exact sample and smoke commands, otherwise it would drift quickly.
- The sample had to prove nested ports and grouped connect syntax without expanding into renderer-owned semantics.

## Lessons Learned

- Product smoke needs explicit waits around model-backed UI surfaces such as Outline.
- A demo sample is only useful when compiler proof, structured smoke, and reviewer usage docs all point at the same workspace and source file.
- Screenshot artifacts are worth keeping only when the cleanup ledger states why they remain and who owns them.
