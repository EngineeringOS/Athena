# Epic 7 Retrospective: Final Closeout, Cleanup, And Regression

## Epic Summary

Epic 7 closed M30 as a milestone rather than a pile of passing stories:

- Story 7.1 recorded the deferred offline QET converter boundary.
- Story 7.2 published the milestone retrospective and cleanup ledger.
- Story 7.3 ran the final purge and sequential M27-M30 regression sweep.

## What Worked

- The QET boundary stayed clean: offline candidate generation only, no runtime dependency, no `.athena` syntax leakage.
- The cleanup ledger gave every retained or deferred artifact an owner, reason, target milestone, and verification path.
- Final regression on M27 through M30 exposed real harness problems that unit tests alone did not catch, and those were fixed before closeout.

## What Needed Discipline

- Story text, smoke assertions, and runtime behavior drifted apart more than once during M30.
- Product smoke hooks need the same rigor as product code; async UI proof collection can invalidate a clean implementation.
- Final purge only works if it treats stale docs and stale proof assumptions as first-class defects.

## Lessons Learned

- Closeout must re-prove the earlier milestones on the final code, not trust intermediate passing runs.
- Cleanup ledgers are only useful if they are kept current when final regressions force contract updates.
- Async proof seams such as Outline collection need deterministic waits, or the smoke harness becomes the bug.
