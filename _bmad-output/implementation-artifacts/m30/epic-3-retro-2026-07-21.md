# Epic 3 Retrospective: Binding Rule Model And Proof Output

## Epic Summary

Epic 3 turned the native symbol pack into executable binding proof:

- Story 3.1 built the binding request/result/compiler contracts.
- Story 3.2 bound demo devices, ports, and folio reference facts into representation occurrences.
- Story 3.3 surfaced missing and ambiguous binding diagnostics and guarded against silent fallback.

## What Worked

- Binding is policy-driven and emits occurrence IR instead of renderer geometry.
- Demo devices bind to native symbols with stable terminal and label bindings.
- Relationship and reference facts are represented as proofed occurrences, not renderer assumptions.
- Diagnostics are deterministic and string-safe for proof payload transport.

## What Needed Care

- Story 3.1 initially missed a policy AC detail: the policy needed an explicit `symbolFamilyId` field.
- Story 3.2 depended on the demo pack staying small and recognizable; expansion would have weakened the proof.
- Story 3.3 needed a strict rejection of silent fallback, not just a positive accepted-path payload.

## Lessons Learned

- Binding stories must validate policy fields as first-class contract data, not as implied renderer choices.
- When a story says "proof payload", the acceptance check should verify the payload shape directly.
- A missing validation field should be recorded as a deliberate deferment only if the story says so explicitly.
- AC-to-evidence review is necessary before `done`; it catches contract omissions that tests can otherwise hide.
