# Epic 4 Retrospective: Composition Intent And Bounds Proof

## Epic Summary

Epic 4 turned representation occurrences into composition proof:

- Story 4.1 defined schematic composition intent facts and anti-CAD transport rules.
- Story 4.2 composed the M30 control-sheet proof with wrapperless normal state and label-safety signals.
- Story 4.3 added structural bounds and regression guards for oversized viewBox, off-screen duplicates, repeated labels, and visible wrapper borders.

## What Worked

- Composition stayed as planning facts instead of collapsing into CAD geometry truth.
- The control-sheet proof records wrapper visibility, label overlap count, route channel count, and reference zone count as explicit proof payload.
- Structural guards now cover the exact regressions that previously broke credibility: large viewBox, duplicate off-screen elements, repeated labels, and visible non-symbol borders.

## What Needed Discipline

- Story 4.2 proves visual credibility through payload facts, not through renderer screenshots alone.
- Story 4.3 confirms that structural invariant checks are the right permanent defense against the earlier SVG/layout regressions.
- Composition stories must resist the temptation to reintroduce geometry truth into semantic or binding layers.

## Lessons Learned

- Visual credibility work still needs machine-checkable invariants.
- Wrapperless normal state should be represented as an explicit proof condition, not a visual preference buried in UI code.
- Bounds proofs are more reliable than eyeballing screenshots for preventing old layout failures from returning.
- The composition layer is useful only if it stays upstream of final geometry and downstream of representation binding.
