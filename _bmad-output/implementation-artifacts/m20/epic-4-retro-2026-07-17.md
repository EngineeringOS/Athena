# M20 Retrospective - Engineering Presentation Fidelity Foundation

Updated: 2026-07-17

## Milestone Review

M20 closed the gap between a correct semantic sheet and a sheet that reads like a professional
engineering artifact.

Across all 12 stories, M20 delivered:

- explicit sheet composition contracts for frame, title block, zones, views, and occurrences
- governed representation families for schematic purpose
- a local proof corpus that reuses the M19 schematic sheet and adds M20 composition facts
- a sheet layout model between Presentation IR and rendering
- deterministic drawing rules for dense content
- repeated-run stability for layout facts
- calmer viewport choreography in Theia
- canonical source, Problems, and sheet coherence through presentation occurrences/connectors
- readability checks at common window sizes
- a customer-facing acceptance fixture
- executable regression coverage for layout and interaction
- explicit deferred boundaries for cabinet preview, repository/import, IEC breadth, and stack choice

## Story-by-Story Summary

### Epic 1 - Sheet Composition

- **1.1 Introduce the sheet composition contract**: the sheet now travels as governed composition
  data instead of renderer-local paint state.
- **1.2 Encode representation families for schematic purpose**: `representationFamilyId` is now
  explicit and fixed to `schematic-sheet` for this proof slice.
- **1.3 Bind the proof corpus to the sheet composition model**: the proof corpus now exercises
  composition facts before render checks.

### Epic 2 - Deterministic Layout

- **2.1 Insert the sheet layout model between Presentation IR and rendering**: layout facts now
  exist as a governed contract.
- **2.2 Encode engineering drawing rules for dense content**: dense placement, spacing, and routing
  rules are now fixture metadata instead of renderer hacks.
- **2.3 Prove repeated runs produce the same layout facts**: repeated projection runs now stay
  stable for both baseline and dense fixtures.

### Epic 3 - Renderer Experience

- **3.1 Stabilize viewport choreography**: fit, zoom, pan, and refocus behavior now preserve manual
  viewport state and remain idempotent.
- **3.2 Keep source, Problems, and sheet coherence intact**: canonical reveal now round-trips
  through presentation occurrences/connectors and falls back cleanly when needed.
- **3.3 Keep dense content readable at common window sizes**: the proof corpus now checks readable
  margins at common desktop sizes.

### Epic 4 - Acceptance And Boundaries

- **4.1 Define the customer-facing acceptance fixture**: the acceptance fixture is a local alias of
  the governed schematic proof, not a new fixture family.
- **4.2 Add executable regression coverage for layout and interaction**: the local regression suite
  now covers acceptance, dense content, and repeatable fit behavior.
- **4.3 Keep the deferred boundaries explicit**: the milestone now has executable guardrails for what
  stays out of scope.

## What Worked

- Keeping semantic authority upstream kept the frontend changes narrow.
- Reusing the M19 schematic corpus prevented the milestone from widening into a new proof family.
- Treating layout as a contract made the renderer easier to reason about.
- The local proof corpus was small enough to review and still broad enough to catch regressions.
- Boundary tests were useful once they checked actual milestone documents and fixtures.

## What Was Hard

- The hardest part was resisting the temptation to turn presentation work into layout-engine
  selection.
- The dense-content slice needed deterministic rules before the viewport work could feel stable.
- Reveal coherence had to read from governed presentation occurrences first, otherwise the frontend
  could drift back toward graph-local guessing.
- The milestone had to stay clear of cabinet preview and ecosystem expansion even when those ideas
  were tempting next steps.

## Key Learnings

1. A professional engineering sheet needs composition, layout, and identity contracts before it
   needs cosmetic refinement.
2. Theia can stay projection-only and still present a credible customer-facing workflow.
3. A small governed proof corpus is more useful than a large demo surface.
4. Deferred boundaries need executable checks, not just PRD text.

## Usage Summary

M20 is used as Athena's first professional engineering-presentation proof:

- open a governed project in the Athena Theia shell
- switch to the schematic sheet surface
- inspect source, Problems, and the sheet through the same canonical subject
- use the acceptance fixture for customer-facing review
- use the dense fixture for readability and layout regression checks
- use the boundary fixture to confirm cabinet preview, repository/import, IEC breadth, and stack
  selection stay deferred

The frontend renders governed projection output. It must not independently reconstruct semantic
meaning from the canvas.
