# Epic 3 Retrospective - M19

Updated: 2026-07-16

## Epic Review

Epic 3 closed the M19 boundary: cabinet preview stays deferred, and ecosystem expansion stays out of the milestone.

M19 as a whole delivered:

- a professional schematic-sheet workflow in Theia
- stable sheet chrome, sheet IR, and deterministic fixture-backed rendering
- canonical selection and reveal round-trips through source, inspector, Problems, and rendered subjects
- executable boundary guardrails for cabinet preview and ecosystem expansion

## What Worked

- Keeping semantic authority upstream made the frontend changes small and testable.
- The existing Theia projection seam was enough for the first customer-visible sheet workflow.
- Story-by-story commits made review and push straightforward.
- Boundary stories were effective once they were backed by executable tests.

## What Was Hard

- The source/diagnostic reveal path needed a clearer failure mode: returning `undefined` on a miss was better than pretending the original diagram was a hit.
- The first boundary assertions needed exact wording from the PRD and addendum, not paraphrased scope labels.
- The worktree already contained unrelated dirty files, so staging had to stay narrow.

## Key Learnings

1. Canonical ids are the right currency for selection, reveal, and boundary checks.
2. Planning docs need executable guardrails when the milestone is mostly about scope discipline.
3. Theia can stay projection-only and still feel like a credible engineering product.

## Usage Summary

M19 is used as the first professional engineering-sheet proof in Athena:

- open a governed project in the Theia product shell
- view the schematic sheet
- inspect source and Problems from the same canonical subject
- verify cabinet preview and ecosystem expansion stay out of scope

The frontend remains downstream of LSP/runtime authority.
